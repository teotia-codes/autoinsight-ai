This dataset belongs to the finance, sales, and business analytics domain.

Primary business objective:
- Understand financial performance and identify the major drivers of revenue, sales, discounts, costs, and profit.
- Discover which products, countries, customer segments, and discount strategies most strongly influence business outcomes.
- Evaluate whether the dataset is suitable for predictive analytics such as profit prediction, sales forecasting, or revenue optimization.

Analytical goals:
- Detect the most important target variable automatically, such as Profit, Sales, or Revenue, depending on the dataset structure.
- Assess data quality issues including missing values, duplicates, outliers, and columns with weak analytical value.
- Identify relationships between numerical business variables such as Sales, Gross Sales, Discounts, Cost of Goods Sold (COGS), Units Sold, and Profit.
- Measure the impact of discounting on sales performance and profitability.
- Compare performance across business dimensions such as Product, Country, Segment, Month, and Year.
- Detect whether the dataset is more suitable for classification or regression tasks.
- Evaluate feature importance for the detected target variable.
- Recommend visualizations that explain business performance clearly and support decision-making.

Expected analytical focus:
- Revenue and profit drivers
- Margin and discount behavior
- Segment-wise and country-wise business performance
- Product-level profitability patterns
- Seasonal or time-based trends if date columns exist
- Outlier detection for unusually high or low sales/profit observations
- Model readiness for business forecasting or predictive analytics

Interpretation rules:
- Avoid overstating weak correlations.
- Treat small correlations as weak unless statistically or practically meaningful.
- Distinguish between revenue growth and profit growth, since high sales do not always imply high profitability.
- Prefer evidence-based conclusions from computed metrics, feature importance, and chart outputs.
- Avoid unsupported claims or business assumptions not grounded in the dataset.
